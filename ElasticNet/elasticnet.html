2.) What is the biological role of DNA methylation? How and where is DNA methylated? ANS: DNA methylation is a process by which methyl groups are added to the DNA molecule. Methylation can change the activity of a DNA segment without changing the sequence. When located in a gene promoter, DNA methylation typically acts to repress gene transcription.

In mammals, DNA methylation is almost exclusively found in CpG dinucleotides, with the cytosines on both strands being usually methylated. The exception is CpG islands, a certain stretches (approximately 1 kilobase in length) where high CpG contents are found.

<Ref: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_methylation>
